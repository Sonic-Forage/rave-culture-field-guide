# DJ and Rave Terminology — The Complete Glossary

> 200+ terms every DJ, raver, producer, and autonomous agent should know. If you're building the future of rave, you need to speak the language.

---

## 🎵 Timing and Music Structure

- **Beat:** The pulse you count. The heartbeat of the track.
- **Bar/Measure:** Usually four beats in common dance music (4/4 time).
- **BPM:** Beats per minute. Tempo. House is ~120-130, techno ~125-140, DnB ~170-180.
- **Downbeat / The One:** First beat of a bar or phrase. Where the kick drum hits hardest.
- **Offbeat:** The spaces between beats. Hi-hats and snares typically live here.
- **Phrase:** A musical sentence, often 8/16/32 bars. DJs mix at phrase boundaries.
- **Breakdown:** Section where drums/energy drop away. The crowd catches their breath.
- **Build:** Rising tension before a drop or return. Could be a filter sweep, riser, snare roll.
- **Drop:** The moment energy returns after a build. The climax. The bass comes back.
- **Fill:** A transitional drum pattern between sections.
- **Intro:** The beginning of a track — usually sparse, designed for mixing.
- **Outro:** The ending — also sparse, designed for mixing out.
- **Loop:** Repeating a section. DJs use loops to extend sections for mixing.
- **Acapella:** Vocal track only, no instruments. Used for mashups and live mixing.
- **Stem:** Individual component of a track (drums, bass, vocals, melody).
- **Sample:** A recorded sound used in a new composition.
- **Hook:** The most memorable part of a song. What gets stuck in your head.

## 🎛️ Mixing and DJ Technique

- **Beatmatching:** Synchronizing the tempo of two tracks so they play in sync. The foundational DJ skill.
- **Sync:** The button that does beatmatching automatically. Controversial. Learn manual first.
- **Mixing / Blending:** Transitioning between two tracks smoothly using volume, EQ, and filters.
- **Crossfader:** A fader that controls the blend between two channels. Left = Deck A, Right = Deck B.
- **Channel fader:** Volume control for individual channels/decks.
- **EQ (Equalization):** Adjusting frequency ranges:
  - **Low / Bass:** The thump. 20-250 Hz.
  - **Mid:** Vocals, melody. 250-4000 Hz.
  - **High / Treble:** Hats, air, brightness. 4000-20000 Hz.
- **Kill:** Fully cutting an EQ band. "Kill the bass" = remove all low frequencies.
- **Filter:**
  - **Low-pass filter (LPF):** Removes highs. Makes things muffled/warm.
  - **High-pass filter (HPF):** Removes lows. Makes things thin/bright.
  - **Sweep:** Gradually moving a filter. Creates tension.
- **Gain / Trim:** Input volume control. Sets the level before it hits the channel.
- **Headroom:** The space between the signal level and maximum (clipping). Keep it.
- **Clipping:** When the signal exceeds maximum. Distortion. Bad. Red lights bad.
- **Cueing:** Listening to the next track in headphones before playing it to the crowd.
- **Cue point:** A marker in a track you can jump to instantly.
- **Hot cue:** A cue point you can trigger while the track is playing.
- **Pitch control / Tempo slider:** Adjusts playback speed (BPM) without changing pitch (on CDJs).
- **Nudge:** Tiny tempo adjustments to keep tracks in sync.
- **Phase:** When two tracks drift in and out of sync. "They're in phase" = locked together.
- **Phrasing:** Aligning your mix to musical phrases so transitions sound natural.
- **Slam / Hard cut:** Switching tracks instantly with no transition. Bold move.
- **Echo out:** Applying echo/delay while fading out. Creates a ghostly tail.
- **Bass swap:** Cutting bass on one track while bringing in the bass of the other.
- **Double drop:** Two bass drops happening simultaneously. DnB staple.
- **Chop:** Quick, percussive cuts between tracks or between a track and silence.
- **Scratch:** Moving a record back and forth to create rhythmic sounds. Turntablism.
- **Beat juggling:** Using two copies of the same record to create new rhythms.

## 🎚️ Equipment and Technology

- **Turntable / Decks:** Record player. The OG. Technics SL-1200 is the gold standard.
- **CDJ:** CD/USB player made by Pioneer. The club standard. CDJ-3000 is current flagship.
- **DJ Controller:** All-in-one device with jog wheels, faders, and a sound card. Connects to laptop.
- **Mixer:** The central hub. Combines multiple audio sources, adds EQ, effects, routing.
- **Pioneer DJM:** The club standard mixer series. DJM-900NXS2, DJM-V10.
- **Serato / Traktor / Rekordbox / VirtualDJ:** DJ software platforms.
- **Ableton Live:** DAW used for live performance and production. Session view = DJ tool.
- **Headphones:** Over-ear, closed-back. For cueing. Sennheiser HD-25 is the industry standard.
- **Monitor speakers:** Speakers in the DJ booth. For previewing.
- **PA system:** The main sound system. What the crowd hears.
- **Subwoofer:** Dedicated bass speaker. Essential for dance music.
- **Amp / Amplifier:** Powers passive speakers.
- **Sound card / Audio interface:** Converts digital audio to analog (and vice versa).
- **MIDI:** Musical Instrument Digital Interface. How controllers talk to computers.
- **DVS (Digital Vinyl System):** Using time-coded vinyl to control digital audio (Serato, Traktor).
- **TB-303:** Roland bass synth. The acid sound. Originally a failed bass guitar replacement.
- **TR-808:** Roland drum machine. The kick drum that defined hip-hop and electronic music.
- **TR-909:** Roland drum machine. The hi-hats and kick that defined house and techno.
- **SP-404:** Roland sampler. Lo-fi, crunchy, hip-hop and beat scene staple.
- **MPC (Akai):** Music Production Center. Sampling, sequencing, finger drumming.
- **Eurorack modular:** Modular synthesizer format. Patching cables, knobs, chaos.
- **Sampler:** Device that records and plays back audio. Hardware or software.
- **Synthesizer:** Generates sound electronically. Analog, digital, FM, wavetable, granular.
- **Drum machine:** Generates drum sounds. Hardware or software.
- **DAW:** Digital Audio Workstation. Software for producing music (Ableton, FL Studio, Logic, etc.).

## 🎶 Genres and Subgenres

### House Family
- **House:** 120-130 BPM. 4/4 kick. Born in Chicago. The foundation.
- **Deep House:** Warm, soulful, jazzy. Larry Heard, Kerri Chandler.
- **Tech House:** Techno + house. Driving, functional. Currently dominant.
- **Progressive House:** Long builds, layered textures. Sasha & Digweed.
- **Afro House:** African rhythms + house. Black Coffee, Culoe de Song.
- **Disco House / Nu-Disco:** Funky, sample-heavy, feel-good.
- **Melodic House:** Emotional, cinematic. Tale of Us, Stephan Bodzin.
- **Acid House:** TB-303 squelch. Phuture, DJ Pierre.
- **Minimal House:** Stripped back, focused on groove. Ricardo Villalobos.
- **Funky House:** Uplifting, vocal, UK-flavored.

### Techno Family
- **Techno:** 125-140 BPM. Hypnotic, industrial, repetitive. Detroit → Berlin.
- **Minimal Techno:** Fewer elements, more space. Robert Hood, Richie Hawtin.
- **Dub Techno:** Echo, delay, reverb. Deepchord, Basic Channel.
- **Industrial Techno:** Noisy, aggressive, dark. Surgeon, Regis.
- **Hard Techno:** 140-160 BPM. Driving, relentless. Kobosil, SPFDJ.
- **Melodic Techno:** Emotional builds, cinematic drops. Tale of Us, ARTBAT.
- **Ambient Techno:** Techno meets ambient. The Orb, Aphex Twin.

### Breakbeat Family
- **Breakbeat:** Non-4/4 rhythms. Funky drums. Florida breaks, nu-skool.
- **Drum & Bass / DnB:** 170-180 BPM. Fast, bass-heavy. Born from jungle.
  - **Liquid DnB:** Smooth, melodic. High Contrast, Calibre.
  - **Neurofunk:** Technical, dark, heavy. Noisia, Ed Rush.
  - **Jump-up:** Bouncy, fun, energetic. DJ Hazard.
  - **Jungle:** Ragga samples, deep bass. The original. Shy FX, Congo Natty.
- **Breakcore:** Extreme breakbeats. Chopped, distorted, chaotic. Venetian Snares.
- **UK Garage:** 130-140 BPM. Skippy drums, 2-step rhythm. Craig David era.

### Bass Music
- **Dubstep:** 140 BPM (half-time). Sub-bass. Started in Croydon. Mala, Skream.
- **Brostep:** Aggressive dubstep. Skrillex. The one that went mainstream.
- **Future Bass:** Melodic, wobbly synths, pop-friendly.
- **Bass House:** House with heavy bass. Jauz, AC Slater.
- **UK Bass:** Umbrella term. Hessle Audio, Livity Sound.
- **Footwork / Juke:** 160 BPM. Chicago. DJ Rashad, RP Boo.
- **Grime:** 140 BPM. UK. MCs over dark electronic beats. Wiley, Stormzy.
- **Trap (EDM):** 808s, hi-hat rolls, hip-hop influenced. Flosstradamus era.

### Trance Family
- **Trance:** 130-150 BPM. Euphoric, melodic, builds and drops. The emotional genre.
- **Psytrance:** 140-150 BPM. Psychedelic, fractal, goatrance roots. Israel, Goa.
- **Progressive Trance:** Deeper, longer, more subtle.
- **Uplifting Trance:** Maximum euphoria. Key changes, soaring melodies.
- **Hard Trance:** Faster, harder. Early 2000s staple.
- **Acid Trance:** TB-303 in a trance context.

### Hardcore Family
- **Happy Hardcore:** 160-180 BPM. Piano stabs, chipmunk vocals, pure joy.
- **Gabber:** 150-200+ BPM. Distorted kicks. Rotterdam. Thunderdome.
- **Hardstyle:** 150 BPM. Distorted kick with a tail. Qlimax, Defqon.1.
- **Frenchcore:** 200+ BPM. Fast, distorted, French. Dr. Peacock.
- **Speedcore:** 300+ BPM. Where does it end? It doesn't.
- **UK Hardcore:** Happy hardcore's modern evolution.

### Downtempo and Ambient
- **Ambient:** No beats, or very sparse beats. Brian Eno, Stars of the Lid.
- **Downtempo:** Slow electronic. Thievery Corporation, Bonobo.
- **Trip-Hop:** Dark, sample-heavy, Bristol. Massive Attack, Portishead, DJ Shadow.
- **Lo-fi Hip-Hop:** Chill beats, vinyl crackle, study music.
- **Chillout:** Catch-all for slow electronic. The rave chillout room.
- **IDM:** Intelligent Dance Music. Aphex Twin, Autechre, Boards of Canada.

### Other Important Genres
- **Electro:** Synth-heavy, funk-influenced. Egyptian Lover, Cybotron.
- **EBM:** Electronic Body Music. Front 242, Nitzer Ebb.
- **Synthwave / Retrowave:** 80s-inspired. Kavinsky, Perturbator.
- **Italo Disco:** Italian disco. Synth-heavy, campy, amazing.
- **Balearic:** No genre rules. Whatever works. Alfredo, Ibiza origins.
- **New Beat:** Belgian. Slow, dark, synth-heavy. Late 80s.

## 👥 Culture and Community

- **PLUR:** Peace, Love, Unity, Respect. The rave code of conduct.
- **Kandi:** Beaded bracelets traded at raves. Each trade is a bond.
- **Gloving:** LED light shows performed with gloves. A rave art form.
- **Shuffling:** Melbourne shuffle. Dance style — gliding footwork.
- **Flow arts:** Poi, levitation wands, hula hoops. Movement as art.
- **Wook / Wook-ism:** Stereotype of a festival-goer — dreadlocks, tie-dye, questionable decisions. Sometimes affectionate, sometimes not.
- **Headbanger:** Someone who aggressively headbangs at dubstep/bass shows.
- **Rail rider:** The person at the very front, pressed against the barrier.
- **K-hole:** Overdissociation from ketamine. Not fun. Be careful.
- **Rolling:** On MDMA. Euphoria, empathy, energy, jaw clenching.
- **Candy raver:** Colorful outfits, lots of kandi, pacifiers. Late 90s aesthetic.
- **Rave mom/dad:** The responsible friend who watches out for everyone.
- **Choon / TUNE:** UK slang for a great track. "That's a choon, mate!"
- **Banger:** An absolute hit. A track that destroys the dance floor.
- **Mashup:** Combining elements of two or more tracks.
- **Bootleg:** An unofficial remix.
- **VIP:** Variation In Production. The artist's own remix of their track.
- **White label:** Unmarked vinyl. No artist or track name. Mystery records.
- **Promo:** Pre-release copy sent to DJs for evaluation.
- **Digging:** Searching for music. Record bins, digital crates, obscure sources.
- **Crates:** A DJ's music collection. "Deep crates" = extensive, eclectic collection.

## 🏢 Venues and Events

- **Warehouse party:** Illegal or semi-legal event in a warehouse. The OG rave venue.
- **Free party:** No admission charge. Sound system collective. Political.
- **Doof:** Australian outdoor rave. Named after the sound: doof doof doof.
- **Teknival:** Free party festival. UK and Europe. Multi-day.
- **Rave cave:** Small, dark, intimate club space.
- **Club night:** A recurring event at a venue. "Fabric on Friday."
- **Residency:** A DJ who plays the same venue regularly. The ultimate trust.
- **Sound system:** A mobile PA rig, often built by a collective. Jamaica → UK.
- **Afters / After-hours:** Party that continues after the main venue closes.
- **Day party:** Daytime event. Brooklyn Mirage, rooftop parties.
- **Silent disco:** Wireless headphones. No noise pollution. Festival staple.

## 💻 Production and Technical

- **DAW:** Digital Audio Workstation. Ableton, FL Studio, Logic, Pro Tools, Bitwig.
- **VST:** Virtual Studio Technology. Plugin instruments and effects.
- **Plugin:** Software that runs inside a DAW.
- **Preset:** A saved setting for a synth or effect.
- **Patch:** A sound programmed on a synthesizer. Also, a cable connection on modular.
- **MIDI:** Musical Instrument Digital Interface. How gear communicates.
- **CC (Continuous Control):** MIDI parameter — knob, fader, pedal.
- **Latency:** Delay between input and output. Enemy of live performance.
- **Buffer size:** Trade-off: lower = less latency, more CPU strain.
- **Sample rate:** How many times per second audio is measured. 44.1kHz = CD quality.
- **Bit depth:** Dynamic range per sample. 16-bit = CD, 24-bit = production standard.
- **Mixdown:** Balancing all elements of a track.
- **Mastering:** Final processing to make a track loud, clear, and consistent.
- **Loudness war:** The race to be louder. Ruined dynamic range. Still happening.
- **Sidechain compression:** Ducking one sound when another plays. "Pumping" effect.
- **Compression:** Reducing dynamic range. Makes quiet parts louder, loud parts quieter.
- **Reverb:** Simulates space. Room, hall, plate, spring.
- **Delay:** Echo effect. Ping-pong, tape, digital.
- **Distortion / Saturation:** Adding harmonics. Warmth, grit, aggression.
- **Chorus / Flanger / Phaser:** Modulation effects. Movement, width.
- **Bitcrusher:** Reduces bit depth for lo-fi, crunchy sounds.
- **Granular synthesis:** Slicing audio into tiny grains and rearranging them.
- **FM synthesis:** Frequency modulation synthesis. Complex, metallic, bell-like.
- **Wavetable synthesis:** Cycling through a table of waveforms. Modern, versatile.
- **Subtractive synthesis:** Starting with harmonics and removing them. Classic.
- **Additive synthesis:** Building sound from individual sine waves.
- **Resampling:** Bouncing audio and re-importing it for further processing.
- **Sound design:** Creating original sounds from scratch.
- **Foley:** Recording real-world sounds for use in music/media.

## 🎤 Performance and Event Terms

- **MC:** Master of Ceremonies. Talks over the music. Hypes the crowd.
- **PA (live):** Live performance. "Ableton live PA" = performing with a laptop.
- **Rider:** A DJ's technical and personal requirements (equipment, hospitality).
- **Green room:** Backstage area for performers.
- **Booth:** The DJ's station. Sacred space.
- **Monitor:** Speakers facing the DJ. For hearing what's going out front.
- **Front of house (FOH):** Where the main sound engineer sits. Facing the stage.
- **FOH engineer:** The person mixing the main speakers.
- **Stage plot:** Diagram showing where equipment goes on stage.
- **Advance:** Pre-event logistics planning. Tech specs, schedules, arrivals.
- **Load-in / Load-out:** Setting up and tearing down.
- **Sound check:** Testing the system before the event.
- **Doors:** When the venue opens to the public.
- **Headliner:** The main act. The draw. Top of the bill.
- **Opener / Warm-up DJ:** First DJ of the night. Sets the mood. Builds slowly.
- **Support:** DJs before the headliner.
- **Closer:** Last DJ. Sends them home.
- **Peak time:** When the dance floor is fullest. The energy peak.
- **Bottle service:** VIP table with expensive bottles. The anti-rave.
- **Pit:** The main dance floor area. Where the energy is.
- **Rooftop:** Elevated outdoor venue. Dope at sunset.
- **Terrace:** Open-air section of a club. Berghain's Panorama Bar has a window.

## 🤖 Autonomous DJ and AI Terms

- **Autonomous DJ:** An AI system that can independently select, mix, and perform music.
- **Ralph Loop:** Sonic-Forage's autonomous DJ loop — detect, select, mix, evaluate, learn.
- **Pilot mode:** AI assists but human makes final decisions.
- **Full auto:** AI makes all decisions. Human monitors.
- **Crowd sensing:** Using cameras, sensors, or biometrics to read the room.
- **Energy mapping:** Tracking crowd energy levels over time.
- **Transition scoring:** Rating how well a mix transition worked.
- **Set arc:** The emotional shape of a DJ set over time.
- **Genre DNA:** The musical characteristics that define a genre.
- **Vibe vector:** Multi-dimensional representation of a track's mood.
- **Bass drop severity scale:**
  - 1/10: Gentle nod
  - 3/10: Subtle head bob
  - 5/10: Full body movement
  - 7/10: Involuntary face melting
  - 9/10: Existential crisis
  - 10/10: Astral projection
