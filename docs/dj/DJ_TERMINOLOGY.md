# DJ and Rave Terminology

## Timing and music structure

- **Beat:** the pulse you count.
- **Bar/measure:** usually four beats in common dance music.
- **BPM:** beats per minute; tempo.
- **Downbeat / the one:** first beat of a bar or phrase.
- **Phrase:** a musical sentence, often 8/16/32 bars.
- **Breakdown:** section where drums/energy drop away.
- **Build:** rising tension before a drop or return.
- **Drop:** moment of impact when rhythm/bass/lead returns.

## Mixing

- **Beatmatching:** aligning tempo and beat position of two tracks.
- **Phrase mixing:** timing transitions so musical sections line up.
- **EQ:** high/mid/low frequency control.
- **Bass swap:** transition move where one track’s low end replaces another’s.
- **Blend:** smooth overlap between tracks.
- **Cut:** fast transition.
- **Loop:** repeated segment.
- **Hot cue:** saved jump point.
- **Sync:** software-assisted tempo/beat alignment.
- **Quantize:** grid-assisted triggering so cues/loops land on beat.

## Gear

- **Deck:** playback surface for one track.
- **Mixer:** blends and shapes multiple audio channels.
- **Channel fader:** volume for one channel.
- **Crossfader:** left-right blend between decks.
- **Gain/trim:** input level before channel volume.
- **Jog wheel:** nudge/scratch/control surface.
- **CDJ/media player:** standalone club playback unit.
- **Controller:** hardware surface controlling DJ software.
- **DVS:** digital vinyl system using timecode vinyl to control digital files.
- **Booth monitor:** speaker aimed at the DJ.

## Sound-system and event terms

- **PA:** public-address sound system for the audience.
- **Rig:** sound system setup.
- **Stack:** speaker stack.
- **Front-of-house:** main mix/sound position in larger venues.
- **Limiter:** device/software preventing unsafe peaks.
- **Redlining:** pushing mixer levels into distortion; do not do this.
- **Load-in/load-out:** moving gear into/out of venue.

## Culture

- **Rave:** dance gathering centered on electronic music, often historically linked to warehouses, fields, secret locations, or immersive club/festival environments.
- **Free party:** DIY/unauthorized or noncommercial party lineage, especially UK sound-system culture.
- **PLUR:** Peace, Love, Unity, Respect.
- **Kandi:** beaded bracelets traded as social tokens in parts of rave/festival culture.
- **Renegade:** unofficial pop-up party, often outdoors or in temporary spaces.
- **Headliner:** main billed act.
- **Opener:** DJ setting the room before peak time.
- **B2B:** back-to-back set where two DJs alternate/select together.
- **Selector:** DJ valued for taste and curation, not just technical tricks.
